Nuts & Dry Fruits

In today's dietary landscape, the consumption of dried fruits has become pervasive. Raisins dominate the market, accounting for almost half of all dried fruit sales, showcasing their enduring popularity. Dates, recognized for their natural sweetness and nutritional benefits, closely follow, along with prunes, figs, apricots, peaches, apples, and pears, contributing to the diverse array of choices available to consumers.

This uptrend is fueled by a growing preference for healthier snacking options, as dried fruits are not only convenient but also offer concentrated flavors and nutritional value. Figs and apricots, with their high fiber content and distinct taste profiles, have particularly gained favor among health-conscious consumers, further solidifying the widespread appeal of dried fruits in contemporary diets. The convenience and extended shelf life of dried fruits also contribute to their popularity, making them a versatile and enduring choice for those seeking both taste and nutrition in their daily diets.

Spices – Safe and Trusty

The spice trade has a rich history that traces back to as early as 2000 BCE, flourishing across the Indian subcontinent and the Middle East. Cinnamon and black pepper played pivotal roles in this trade in these regions, while East Asia contributed with a variety of herbs and pepper. The demand for these exotic spices and herbs became a catalyst for the expansion of world trade during this era.

Spices serve a myriad of culinary and medicinal purposes and can be utilized in various forms. Whether used fresh and whole, or subjected to methods such as drying, grating, chopping, crushing, or grinding, spices impart distinctive flavors to a wide range of dishes. Additionally, extraction into tinctures became another method, showcasing the versatility of spices in traditional medicine and culinary arts alike. This rich heritage of the spice trade continues to influence global cuisines and cultural exchanges to this day.
